Travel Tips

01

Bring a small backpack/bag

By purchasing a small backpack, you will be forced to pack light and avoid carrying too much stuff.

02

Travel by yourself at least once

You’ll learn a lot about yourself and how to become independent. It’s a cliché, but it’s true. Traveling solo taught me how to fend for myself, talk to people, and handle unfamiliar situations with ease.

03

Make extra copies of your passport and important documents.

Don’t forget to e-mail a copy to yourself too. You never know when you might need to have some sort of documentation.

04

Learn basic phrases in the native language of your destination

The locals will appreciate it and it will make your interactions easier.

05

Try new food.

Don’t ask what it is. Just put it in your mouth and see if you like it. If you put your guard up, you might miss out on some unusual and delicious local cuisine.

06

Take pictures of your luggage and clothes.

f your bag gets lost, this will help identify it more easily and speed up the process of having your travel insurance reimburse you.

07

Be patient.

Things will work out in the end. No need to rush. You’ll get to where you are going in due time. Travel is about the journey, not the destination.

08

Be respectful.

Locals are willing to help you out, but there’s probably a language barrier, so keep your cool when something doesn’t go your way.

09

Don’t over plan your trip.

Let your days unfold naturally. Schedule two or three things and let the day fill in the rest on its own.
